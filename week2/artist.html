Olafur Eliasson

Biography

Olafur Eliasson is a Danish-Icelandic artist known for his large-scale installation artworks. He was born in 1967 in Copenhagen, Denmark. His artworks often explore light, color, water, and nature. Eliasson creates interactive installations that allow people to experience space and environment in a new way. One of his most famous works is The Weather Project, which was displayed at the Tate Modern museum in London. His art encourages people to think about nature and climate change. Olafur Eliasson combines science, design, and art to create meaningful experiences for viewers. Today, he is one of the most influential contemporary artists in the world.
